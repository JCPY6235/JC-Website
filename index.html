Model Reduction for PDEs with Lorentz Cone Constraints (Preprint)

Submitted paper in SISC journal

Authors: Sagbo Jean-Claude Mitchozounou

We propose a novel reduced-order modeling approach for parametrized varia- tional inequalities applied to contact mechanics. Traditional reduced-basis methods for variational inequalities rely on mixed primal–dual formulations, which face dif- ficulties in dual reduction, due to the requirement to preserve the non-negativity of dual variables. To address these challenges, we propose an exclusively primal formulation that circumvents dual cone compression. The resulting reduced model guarantees feasibility with respect to inequality constraints and offers substantial computational savings without compromising accuracy. The proposed reduction strategy is applied to elastic frictionless contact mechanics under small deforma- tions with matching meshes. Numerical experiments are presented to demonstrate the robustness and efficiency of the proposed method.
